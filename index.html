NLP, Named Entity Recognition

Python package: fastner

NER

Named Entity Recognition is one of the most important tasks in information extraction, but training a model to the state of the art based on transformers is not immediate (requires defining a class for the Dataset, Trainer, etc..). To simplify the whole process, I developed fastner.
fastner is a Python package to finetune transformer-based models for the Named Entity Recognition task in a simple and fast way (only a few lines of code!).

PyPI:pypi.org/project/fastner
GitHub:github.com/vittoriomaggio/fastner

Year: 2022

NLP, Weak supervision

Weak Supervision approaches for Named Entity Recognition

NER Weak Supervision

Over the last few years, the amount of textual information has rapidly increased. Named Entity Recognition is one of the most valuable and popular tasks to extract information from these texts. The main approaches to perform this task are: rulebased, machine learning, and hybrid approaches. In this thesis, I focus on supervised machine learning approaches, where the main problem of the traditional technique is that they don’t take into account the noise of the supervision while, in the real world, we have to deal with noisy labels (because of annotator errors, lack of information or because tags were automatically extracted from knowledge bases). I analyze the state-of-the-art techniques found in the literature; I conduct experiments using a noise-robust loss function and propose an extension of it, demonstrating an improvement in F1 score compared both with the original loss and the cross-entropy loss (usually used for this task).

Year: 2022

NLP, Knowledge representation

American Election 2016 Tweets Analysis

NER (DBpedia) NEL (DBpedia) BERT GloVe W2V

In recent years the use of social media has become increasingly central to people's social lives, becoming decisive even in the world of politics and propaganda. Therefore, in this project is proposed an analysis of the use of Tweets by political leaders Donald Trump and Hillary Clinton during the election campaign for the 2016 US presidential election. The Named Entity Recognition and Named Entity Linking task was performed to investigate the entities mentioned by the two political leaders and to be able to identify discussion topics (inferred from the entity analysis). We then proceeded to study the position taken by the two leaders with respect to these topics through polarization analysis.

Complex System

Optimization of Bots position on Twitter for maximizing spread news

Autonomous Agent SOIL Network science

In this project, we will introduce a multi-agent-based method to find the best position of the bots (definited as a node in a social graph that is an automatic or nonautomatic system that relaunches news to expose the higher possible number of nodes that are users of the graph at the news) in a social network graph for maximizing the number of users exposed to the news. The case study is based on the Twitter account of the ex-president of the council Giuseppe Conte.

Data Analytics

Amazon reviews and products analysis

Sentiment Analysis Network Analysis Data Analysis

Analysis on reviews of products sold on amazon in the entertainment industry. In the first part, an in-depth analysis on the sentiment of reviews for each product category was performed (wordcloud, sentiment distribution over the years, correlation between price and sentiment, usefulness of reviews for users). In the second part, a network analysis was performed on the product graph to identify the most popular products. Through community detection algorithms the most relevant products for each sub-category were identified.
